# Brainstorming Session Results

**Facilitator:** Hankeol
**Date:** 2026-04-15

## Session Overview

**Topic:** Korean Government Ideathon — building on Cherry/KaaS to propose an idea where agents improve citizens' daily lives
**Goals:** Generate compelling ideathon proposals that lead to funding, government partnership, and PR opportunities

### Context

**Core Idea:** Cherry/KaaS is an **Agent Learning Resource Market** — a marketplace where AI agents acquire skills and knowledge (curated by human experts) to operate more reliably. Blockchain provenance tracks what knowledge agents consume. MCP protocol makes it plug-and-play for any agent.

**Ideathon Context:**
- Hosted by: Korean government
- Theme: Using agents to improve daily life
- Success = funding, government partnership, PR
- Current government direction: Encouraging citizens to propose ideas and start small businesses

### Key Insight

The brainstorming converged on one core framing: **"Connecting verified public knowledge to citizens' personal agents (secretaries)."** The idea is NOT to reframe Cherry/KaaS for the public sector, but to build on top of it — extending the existing platform with a public knowledge curator economy.

---

## Emerged Direction: A + C Strategy

**Pitch Structure:**

> **WHY (A — Infrastructure):** "The AI agent era requires public knowledge infrastructure" — Korea needs verified, structured knowledge that citizens' agents can consume. Just as roads, electricity, and internet were national infrastructure, verified knowledge is the next infrastructure layer.
>
> **WHAT (C — Curator Economy):** "We're building the curator economy that makes it sustainable" — Our contribution is the economic layer where experts, citizens, and AI collaborate to curate, verify, and distribute public knowledge, with blockchain-backed provenance and fair compensation.

**Why this works:** Government sees the need (A) but we're proposing something we can actually execute (C). Cherry already proves the AI+human hybrid model works.
